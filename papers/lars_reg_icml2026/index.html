Layer-Adaptive Regularization Scheduling: A Unified Framework for Training Stability in Deep Networks

A. Kobayashi, R. Nishimura, T. Fujita, M. Endo

Proceedings of the 43rd International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2026

OPEN REVIEW — Weak Reject

Abstract

本論文では、深層ニューラルネットワークの学習安定性を向上させるための統一的フレームワークとして、Layer-Adaptive Regularization Scheduling（LARS-Reg）を提案する。提案手法は、各層の勾配ノルムの移動分散を監視し、その変動に応じてドロップアウト率とWeight Decayを動的に調整する。CIFAR-10/100、Tiny-ImageNet、および表形式データセットを用いた評価において、提案手法は固定ドロップアウト、AutoDropout、Scheduled DropPathと比較して一貫した改善を示し、深層ResNetにおいて最大2.3%の精度向上を達成した。

1. Introduction

Regularization is essential for training deep neural networks, preventing overfitting and improving generalization [1, 2]. However, the optimal regularization strength varies across layers and throughout the training process [3]. Existing adaptive regularization methods such as AutoDropout [4] and Scheduled DropPath [5] adjust regularization strength based on validation performance or predefined schedules, but do not leverage layer-specific gradient information.

In this work, we propose Layer-Adaptive Regularization Scheduling (LARS-Reg), which monitors the gradient norm variance at each layer and dynamically adjusts both dropout rate and weight decay accordingly.

2. Method

2.1 Gradient Norm Variance as Stability Signal

We define the gradient norm variance for layer l at iteration t as σ²l(t) = Var(||∇W_lℒ||²), computed over a sliding window of size W.

2.2 Regularization Adjustment

For each layer, the dropout rate pl and weight decay λl are updated as: pl(t+1) = pl(t) + ηp·(σ²l(t)−τ). Hyperparameters: ηp = 0.002, W = 50, τ = threshold.

4. Limitations

(1) 理論的正当性：勾配ノルム分散を「層の不安定性」の代理指標として用いる理論的裏付けが不十分であり、分散増大が過学習の兆候である保証はない。(2) ハイパーパラメータηp=0.002, W=50の選定根拠が感度分析のみに留まる。(3) Transformer系アーキテクチャでの検証未実施。(4) 多重比較補正未実施。

5. Conclusion

LARS-Reg provides a computationally efficient approach to adaptive regularization by leveraging gradient statistics already computed during backpropagation. While the approach has limitations in theoretical grounding and experimental coverage, it offers a practical mechanism for improving training stability.
